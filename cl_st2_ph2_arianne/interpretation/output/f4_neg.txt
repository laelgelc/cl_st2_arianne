Interpretation of Factor f4 (negative pole)

1. Which texts and groups drive this pole?

- The **most negative scores** (i.e. strongest presence of this pole) in the examples all come from **persona_gemini** texts.
- In the mean scores, however, **human texts are overall lowest on f4 (neg)** (mean 8.19 vs. Gemini 2.91, Grok 3.26, GPT 6.88).  
  Since we are interpreting the **negative pole**, the groups with **lower mean scores** are more associated with this pole. So:
  - **Most associated with f4_neg overall:** persona_gemini and persona_grok (lowest means)
  - **Least associated with f4_neg overall:** human and persona_gpt (highest means)

So this pole is primarily an **LLM–persona (especially Gemini) style** of discourse, not the dominant human Greenpeace style.

4. Discourse characteristics of this pole

Combining the loadings and examples, this pole encodes a discourse that is:

- **Systemic and structural**  
  Focus on **systems** (industrial agriculture, global food systems, transport systems, capitalism, inequality) rather than localised events or purely ecological description.

- **Institutionally oriented**  
  Frequent reference to **formal institutions and governance arenas** (UN, FAO, World Economic Forum, ministries, institutes, associations, councils, forums) as key sites of power and change.

- **Macro-economic and redistributive**  
  Heavy emphasis on **taxation, finance, economics, inequality/equality, debt, subsidies** as levers for climate and social justice (wealth taxes, kerosene taxes, ending tax breaks, dividend bans, taxing polluters).

- **Justice- and movement-framed**  
  Climate and environmental issues are framed through **social justice, food sovereignty, Indigenous rights, grassroots movements**, and campaigns like “Make Amazon Pay”.

- **Abstract and policy-focused**  
  The lexis is **nominal and abstract** (tax, inequality, innovation, reduction, politics, finance) and often tied to **policy prescriptions** and **global governance debates**, rather than concrete local actions alone.

5. Proposed labels for f4 (negative pole)

Any label should capture: (i) structural/systemic framing, (ii) institutional/governance focus, (iii) economic-justice levers.

Possible labels:

1. **“Structural Economic‑Justice Governance Discourse”**  
   - “Structural”: captures the systemic, macro-level framing (capitalism, industrial systems, inequality).  
   - “Economic‑Justice”: foregrounds tax, finance, inequality, wealth redistribution.  
   - “Governance”: reflects the centrality of UN, FAO, ministries, forums, councils, associations.

2. **“Institutional Climate‑Justice and Fiscal Reform Discourse”**  
   - “Institutional”: for the dense institutional lexicon (UN, FAO, institutes, ministries, forums).  
   - “Climate‑Justice”: signals the integration of climate with social and economic justice.  
   - “Fiscal Reform”: highlights taxation, subsidies, debt, and financial levers as core themes.

3. **“Systemic Policy‑and‑Tax Justice Discourse”**  
   - “Systemic”: for the focus on whole systems (food, transport, global economy).  
   - “Policy‑and‑Tax”: captures the concrete levers repeatedly invoked (bans, regulations, taxes, subsidies).  
   - “Justice”: reflects the recurring inequality/equality, Indigenous rights, food sovereignty framing.

Justification

- The **loadings** point strongly to **institutions, macro-economics, and inequality** (institute, ministry, un, intergovernmental, council, forum, tax, finance, economics, inequality, equality, politics, reduction).
- The **examples** show these words embedded in **critiques of capitalism and global governance**, and in **programmatic calls for wealth taxes, kerosene taxes, ending subsidies, debt cancellation, and systemic reforms**.
- The pole is **driven mainly by LLM persona texts (especially Gemini)**, suggesting a stylised, perhaps slightly over-systematised version of Greenpeace’s justice-oriented discourse, but still clearly anchored in **institutional, economic, and justice-focused climate politics**.

Among the options, the most encompassing and neutral label for this pole is:

**Structural Economic‑Justice Governance Discourse**.
