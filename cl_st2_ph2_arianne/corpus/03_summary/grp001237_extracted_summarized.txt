The blog post highlights recent global bans on plastics to combat environmental pollution. France banned thin single-use plastic bags as part of an EU effort. Morocco implemented a comprehensive ban on plastic bag production, import, sale, and distribution, facing stockpiling resistance. India's Karnataka state banned various plastics like bags, plates, and microbeads, seizing 39,000kg of illegal plastic. San Francisco banned plastic bags (2007), water bottles (2014), and styrofoam, which is hard to recycle. Other examples include Honolulu's 2015 bag ban, Tasmania's 2003 reduction of 350,000 bags, and Ethiopia's 2011 ban tied to green energy. Plastic ocean pollution harms marine life; Greenpeace urges joining the Plastics Pledge.
