Six-year-old Lucas suffers from allergic rhinitis, causing nasal congestion, runny nose, and daily nosebleeds, likely due to air pollution from heavy traffic near his Hong Kong apartment. His mother, Margret, wipes soot-covered windows multiple times daily and keeps them closed. They joined Greenpeace Hong Kong's Citizen Science Research, installing tubes to collect NO2 samples, revealing poor air quality. NO2 irritates mucous membranes and hinders respiratory development. Nine of 16 monitoring stations exceeded national standards last year. The government must curb private car growth and electrify public transport to protect health.
