The blog post highlights air pollution from fossil fuels as a public health emergency, causing 4.5 million annual deaths worldwide, while coal, oil, and car companies profit. It also links fossil fuels to climate change and criticizes insufficient action by companies and governments. Inspiring community examples include: crowdfunding for solar installations at seven Thai hospitals in 2019 to reduce fossil fuel use; 31 Jakarta citizens suing the government, prompting petitions and promises from the governor; and a Belgian mother's initiative monitoring school air quality, sparking nationwide street closures and influencing elections. It urges signing petitions and organizing events.
