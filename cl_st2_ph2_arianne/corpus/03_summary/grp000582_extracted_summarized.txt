The blog post critiques end-of-year consumption spikes during events like Black Friday, Singles Day, and Cyber Monday, driven by low prices that overlook environmental costs. It notes that 55% of the world's population lives in cities, projected to reach 70% by 2050, with urban consumption causing 70% of global greenhouse gas emissions. Governments promote consumerism amid COVID-19 recovery, boosting e-commerce. The post urges consuming less and better, analyzing purchases to reduce high-carbon impacts from production, shipping, and packaging. It advocates city actions like banning ads, promoting repair cafes, reuse, and sharing, while holding e-commerce accountable for sustainable practices.
