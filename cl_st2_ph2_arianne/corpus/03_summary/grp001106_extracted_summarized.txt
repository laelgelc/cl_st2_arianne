The blog post highlights the devastating oil spills by Lukoil in Russia's Pechora river region, poisoning rivers, killing fish, and harming Indigenous communities. Local resident Galina Chuprova compares the company to an invading enemy, urging villagers to unite in defense. Greenpeace volunteer Julia Lapshina describes witnessing toxic spills and questions oil producers' disregard for safety. The Save Pechora Committee and Greenpeace demand reduced spills and higher standards. They organized a 'Skis against oil' race to rally locals for the Break Free from Fossil Fuels movement. Committee members Dmitriy Kanev and Fyodor Terentiev share motivations rooted in protecting nature and traditional ways, emphasizing collective action's power.
