The blog post highlights PFC pollution from DuPont facilities, sharing stories of Dutch couple Jan and Ineke van Genderen, who have elevated PFOA blood levels near the Dordrecht plant, and US residents Gwen and Earl Botkin, affected by Ohio River contamination. It details a class action lawsuit exposing DuPont's cover-up of PFOA's health risks, linked to diseases like high cholesterol, cancers, and thyroid issues. Greenpeace partners for protests, hearings, and investigations, noting PFOA's replacement with hazardous shorter-chain PFCs and urging support to combat toxic chemicals.
