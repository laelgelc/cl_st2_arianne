The blog post, part of the “LIVE from the Pacific” series, describes provisioning food for 25 people on a three-month Greenpeace ship voyage, with stops in Curacao and Panama for fresh ingredients, followed by unpacking and storing. The author, from Taiwan and new to sea life, discusses coping with seasickness through fresh air, breathing techniques, pills, and avoiding caffeine, aided by onboard medic Loes. It recounts big waves causing kitchen chaos and sleep disruptions on bunk beds. The French chef Laurence prepares healthy meals, with volunteers assisting in the galley despite rolling conditions, including waste sorting.
