The blog post envisions a transformed world by 2032, prioritizing people and the planet over profit and growth, inspired by Indigenous concepts like considering impacts on seven future generations and the Ubuntu principle of interconnected humanity. It highlights recent crises, such as COVID-19 exposing reliance on caregivers and the Ukraine war revealing flaws in food and energy systems dependent on fossil fuels. Greenpeace's Alternative Futures programme amplifies solutions from the global majority, challenges colonial and capitalist inequalities, and calls for a just transition to renewables, equitable societies, and harmony with nature.
