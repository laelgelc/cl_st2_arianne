The blog post commemorates the 1969 Stonewall riots in New York, where Trans Women, Drag Queens, and sex workers like Marsha P. Johnson and Sylvia Rivera resisted oppression, sparking global change for Queer rights. It explains Pride Month as a celebration of non-heterosexual value, noting 71 countries still criminalize same-sex activity. The author, a conservation worker, urges recognition of Queer environmental activism amid climate change vulnerabilities, highlighting groups like Queers x Climate and emphasizing that social and environmental justice are inseparable. It calls for advocating Earth's protection during Pride Month, 53 years later.
