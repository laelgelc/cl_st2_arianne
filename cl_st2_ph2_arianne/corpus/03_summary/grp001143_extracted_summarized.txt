The blog post ranks Antarctic penguins in a countdown for Penguin Awareness Day, highlighting their adaptations while promoting a Greenpeace campaign for an Antarctic Ocean Sanctuary. Chinstrap penguins appear squat and serious, resembling helmet-wearers. Macaroni penguins, monogamous crested cousins of Rockhoppers, nest on the Antarctic Peninsula and feed on krill. Gentoo penguins feature white head patches, brush-like tails, loud trumpeting calls, and swim at speeds up to 36 km/h, diving hundreds of times daily. Emperor penguins, the largest, dive deepest, use bubbles for hydrodynamics, and huddle as dads to protect eggs in winter.
