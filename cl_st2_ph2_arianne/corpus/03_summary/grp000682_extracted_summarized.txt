The Saya de Malha Bank in the Indian Ocean, part of the Mascarene Plateau connecting Seychelles, Mauritius, and RÃ©union, hosts diverse wildlife including pygmy blue whales, sperm whales, flying fish, and tuna. It features the world's largest seagrass meadow, a major carbon sink that stores carbon to slow climate breakdown and supports species like dugongs and tiger sharks. Recognized as an Ecologically and Biologically Significant Area under shared Seychelles-Mauritius governance, with international waters. Greenpeace's Arctic Sunrise expedition mapped wildlife using binoculars, hydrophones, and eDNA from water samples. Amid ocean pressures like 71% shark decline from bycatch, it calls for ocean sanctuaries and a Global Ocean Treaty.
