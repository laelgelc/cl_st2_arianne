The climate crisis unequally affects millions, threatening future generations, while voices of those most impacted—especially Indigenous communities—are often ignored. The post highlights young activists who spoke out at COP26, including Yared Abera from Ethiopia, Samara Assunção from Brazil, Hindou Oumarou Ibrahim from Chad, India Logan-Riley from Aotearoa, Ryan Bestre from the Philippines, Ineza Umuhoza Grace from Rwanda, Txai Suruí from Brazil, Marinel Ubaldo from the Philippines, Vanessa Nakate from Uganda, Leala Pourier from the Pine Ridge Indian Reservation, Aisha Akbar from Pakistan, and Maytik Avirama from Colombia. They demanded commitments and action, using their unique perspectives against intimidation.
