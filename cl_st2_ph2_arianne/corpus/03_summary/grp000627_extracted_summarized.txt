The remnants of Hurricane Ida brought record-breaking rain, flash floods, and a tornado to the Northeast US, affecting New York, New Jersey, Connecticut, Pennsylvania, and Maryland, with over 40 deaths and disproportionate impacts on marginalized communities. The author describes personal experiences of minor flooding in Brooklyn and checking on loved ones. Officials linked the storm to the fossil fuel-driven climate crisis, amid global extremes like fires, heat waves, and floods. The IPCC report signals a "code red for humanity," urging an end to fossil fuel subsidies and a just transition to renewables.
