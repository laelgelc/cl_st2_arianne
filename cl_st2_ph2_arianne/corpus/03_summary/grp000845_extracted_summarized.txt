The blog post highlights the 2020 Olympics and Paralympics in Japan amid the ongoing Fukushima nuclear crisis, criticizing Prime Minister Abe's 2013 claim that the situation is under control. It discusses challenges like managing over 1 million tonnes of contaminated water, 880 tonnes of molten nuclear fuel, environmental contamination, impacts on workers and citizens, and human rights issues. An HBO documentary raises questions about hosting the games during this disaster, government transparency, and safety. Greenpeace applauds Olympic values, urges the IOC to ensure environmental sustainability and safety, notes radiation hotspots in Fukushima areas for events and torch relay, and announces upcoming radiation survey results.
