The COP15 UN Biodiversity Conference concluded with the Kunming-Montreal Agreement, hailed as historic but merely a starting point to halt mass extinction. It recognizes Indigenous Peoples, who protect 80% of Earth's biodiversity despite comprising 5% of humanity, emphasizing respect for their rights, territories, consent, and participation per the UN Declaration. Indigenous-led conservation must become standard. The agreement includes a 30% protection target for land and seas by 2030, but lacks exclusions for damaging activities and incorporates corporate schemes like nature-based solutions, risking exploitation. Finance commitments remain inadequate, requiring faster action, including a 2023 fund for developing countries and Indigenous Peoples, plus a Global Ocean Treaty.
