The blog post reflects on the 10th anniversary of the Fukushima nuclear disaster, triggered by a tsunami, comparing it to historical events like 9/11, the 2004 tsunami, and Chernobyl. It highlights the ongoing trauma for survivors, mass evacuations, and spreading radiation contamination. Reviewing Greenpeace archives from 2011 to 2019, it notes that a decade is insufficient to erase the pain. The post presents visual documentations of radiation in Japanese prefectures, emphasizing Greenpeace's role in bearing witness to prevent forgetting and advocate for a nuclear-free world.
