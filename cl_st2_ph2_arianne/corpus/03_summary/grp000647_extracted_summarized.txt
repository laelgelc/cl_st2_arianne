In Bolsonaro's Brazil, Indigenous Peoples' rights and lands face threats from the Marco Temporal loophole, which could legalize land theft by requiring proof of occupation during the 1988 constitution, and bill PL 490, which aims to open Indigenous territories for industrial agriculture and mining. This endangers isolated communities and ignores centuries of Indigenous presence. Greenpeace supports defending these rights, emphasizing Indigenous Peoples as forest guardians.
