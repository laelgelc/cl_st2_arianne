The text explores the intersection of gender inequality, human rights, and the climate crisis, stressing the need for girls' education to enable women's participation in climate decision-making. It highlights women's economic disadvantages in the patriarchal fossil fuel industry, including pay gaps and disproportionate impacts from rising energy costs and pollution, which exacerbate vulnerability to gender-based violence and extreme weather events. Activists note that women support renewable energy more than men and that intersectional approaches are essential to mitigate these effects and empower women in leadership roles.
