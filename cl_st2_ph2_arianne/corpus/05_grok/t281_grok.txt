Wildfires are escalating around the world, ravaging regions from Turkey and Greece to Russia, Brazil, the US, Canada, Italy, and Lebanon. These fires are fueled by climate change, extreme heat, and drought, destroying forests, wildlife, and vital carbon storage. The blazes are worsening emissions and air quality, compounding challenges during the COVID-19 pandemic.

In the Amazon, illegal fires are burning under Bolsonaro's administration. Massive wildfires in the US and Canada are producing smoke that travels to distant areas.

Greenpeace is calling for phasing out fossil fuels, ending subsidies, protecting forests with guidance from Indigenous peoples, and urgent climate action. This push coincides with the IPCC's report highlighting fossil fuels' role in weather extremes.
