Yewande Omotoso discusses climate change as a human rights crisis threatening rights to life, property, health, and culture. She highlights climate justice as a global movement using legal action to hold fossil fuel corporations and governments accountable. Omotoso describes inspiring cases, including the Philippines' investigation of "Carbon Majors," Swiss seniors challenging inadequate climate policies, South Africa's ruling against a coal plant, Colombian youth protecting the Amazon, and U.S. youth suing the government. Omotoso shares how these efforts combat overwhelm, inspiring action against the climate "monster."
