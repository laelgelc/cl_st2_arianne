In the vibrant landscape of Thailand's environmental movement, women are leading the charge, wielding influence across social, political, and economic spheres to drive meaningful change. Their stories inspire us all, showcasing the power of dedication and resilience in protecting our planet. Here, we profile six remarkable Thai environmental activists whose work continues to make a profound impact.

Supaporn Malailoy stands at the forefront of advocating for environmental human rights, tirelessly campaigning for essentials like clean air and water that every community deserves.

Panita Pook Kongsook, a dedicated long-time Greenpeace volunteer, champions non-violent action, mobilizing people to address environmental challenges through peaceful and effective means.

Sureerat Taechutrakul played a pivotal role in halting the construction of coal plants in Prachuap Khiri Khan, demonstrating the strength of community-led resistance against harmful energy projects.

Nanticha “Lynn” Ocharoenchai founded Climate Strike Thailand, galvanizing youth and citizens to pressure the government on urgent climate issues and push for systemic reforms.

Pornchita Fapratanprai has been a fierce opponent of coal mining in her indigenous community, safeguarding traditional lands and ways of life from destructive industrial encroachment.

Gig Koranis Tanangsnakool conducts research and uses social media to promote alternatives to single-use plastics, educating and inspiring broader adoption of sustainable practices.

These women embody courage and passion for their communities and the environment, reminding us that individual actions can spark collective transformation.
