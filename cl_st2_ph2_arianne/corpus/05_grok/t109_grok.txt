As a photo editor sifting through images of global climate disasters, the mental health toll is profound. These visuals capture the human suffering amplified by climate change, leaving a lasting emotional impact.

Recent events highlight the crisis: devastating floods in Pakistan have submerged a third of the country, displacing millions, even though the nation contributes low emissions. In Bangalore, India, unprecedented rains have caused widespread damage, compounded by poor planning. Flooding in West and Central Africa has displaced over 125,000 people, with 300 deaths reported in Nigeria. Europe faces its worst drought, drying up rivers. In Chile, an ongoing drought, worsened by water privatization, affects half the population. Typhoons and hurricanes have struck Asia, Puerto Rico, and Canada.

Amid growing frustration with fossil fuel profits, youth marches provide a beacon of hope, pushing for an end to dependence on these fuels.
