The 2020 Greenpeace film festival is showcasing 15 free documentaries that delve into pressing environmental issues, including climate, food, agriculture, overconsumption, biodiversity, whistleblowers, and fossil energies. These films are available to watch in both English and French, giving audiences around the world the chance to engage with vital stories.

In this unique festival, viewers take on the role of the jury by voting for the People's Choice Award, helping to highlight the most impactful film.

The lineup explores a range of topics, such as the destruction of forests in Borneo, efforts to rewild landscapes across Europe, the environmental impact of sea freight, initiatives to save the Amazon, challenges faced by rural communities in France, the preservation of oceans, broader planetary crises, Indigenous resistance to oil development in New Zealand, plastic pollution affecting beaches in the Basque region, the connection between eggs and African migration, threats to endangered forests in France, climate refugees from Kiribati, fights against climate change in the U.S., actions individuals can take daily to support biodiversity, and the reform of industrial agriculture.
