Climate change disproportionately impacts women, yet this very challenge empowers them to lead the climate justice movement through storytelling and activism.

Joanna Sustento, a survivor of Typhoon Haiyan who lost family members in the disaster, advocates for holding carbon companies accountable. She emphasizes that safe environments are a human right.

Desiree Llanos Dee uses personal stories to humanize the effects of climate change, connect global communities, and build collective action.

Greenpeace sailor Hettie amplifies the voices of vulnerable people through ship tours, such as in Tacloban.

We celebrate women's resilience and urge support for their fight against climate injustice.
