Across Asia, Greenpeace volunteers are united by a shared passion for climate justice, and they're sharing inspiring stories of their activism. Ronan from the Philippines became an advocate after experiencing Typhoon Haiyan, focusing on storytelling and policy work to drive change. Hui Ling from Malaysia founded the local Greenpeace office and leads campaigns against pollution and deforestation. Tya from Indonesia combats forest fires as a volunteer firefighter. Amika from Thailand addresses plastic waste and the impacts of climate change. Minseop from South Korea designs visuals to push for climate policy changes following floods. Neha from India promotes composting and clean air initiatives. Together, they highlight the importance of resilience, direct action, and community involvement in protecting the environment.
