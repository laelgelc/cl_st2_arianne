As we reflect on the environmental progress made in 2022, it's inspiring to see a series of significant victories that advance the fight for a healthier planet and stronger protections for communities.

In the Philippines, the Commission on Human Rights made a groundbreaking declaration, recognizing climate change as a human rights issue and emphasizing the accountability of corporations in addressing it.

The European Union took a stand by proposing legislation to combat Strategic Lawsuits Against Public Participation (SLAPPs), aimed at shielding environmental advocates from legal harassment.

At the global level, the United Nations General Assembly passed a resolution affirming the universal right to a clean, healthy, and sustainable environment.

In California, a new ban was implemented on drilling new oil and gas wells near homes, schools, and other community areas, prioritizing public health.

The EU also advanced forest protection with a law mandating traceability in supply chains to prevent products linked to deforestation from entering the market.

Canada's decision to reject the expansion of an iron mine in the Arctic helped safeguard Inuit lands and traditional ways of life.

There were widespread international calls for a moratorium on deep sea mining to preserve fragile ocean ecosystems.

The UN furthered efforts against pollution with a resolution paving the way for a global treaty on plastics.

Legal triumphs included court rulings in Argentina halting oil exploration, in South Africa upholding the right to clean air, and in Indonesia blocking further oil activities.
