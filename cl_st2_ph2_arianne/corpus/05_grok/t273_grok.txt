Fossil fuel companies are increasingly relying on soft power tactics, such as sponsorships and strategic influence, to greenwash their images and protect their business models that continue to harm the climate. By associating themselves with popular cultural, educational, and sporting events, these corporations attempt to distract from their environmental impact and maintain public support.

Take the world of rugby, for instance, where companies like Ineos and Total have stepped in as sponsors for major events. This allows them to polish their reputations while their core operations contribute to climate damage. Similarly, Shell has funded museum exhibitions, but with clauses that prevent any criticism of the company, effectively silencing potential scrutiny. ExxonMobil has also poured money into the arts, all while publicly denying the science of climate change.
