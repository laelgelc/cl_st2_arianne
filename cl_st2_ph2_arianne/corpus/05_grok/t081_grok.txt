Indigenous Peoples make up less than 5% of the global population but protect 80% of the world's biodiversity, even as they face rights violations and exclusion from decision-making processes.

At COP15, Indigenous leaders are sharing their experiences and calling for change. Valentin Engobo from the Democratic Republic of Congo emphasizes the role of community forestry and recent discoveries of peatlands that store vast amounts of carbon. Orpha from West Papua is fighting against oil palm plantations that clear forests without community consent.
