As we celebrate World Photography Day, it's a powerful reminder of how images can capture the urgency of environmental crises and inspire action. In 2019, Greenpeace photographers documented critical moments around the globe, shining a light on the threats facing our planet and the people fighting to protect it.

One striking image shows activists scaling an oil rig off the coast of Norway, protesting against Arctic drilling and the risks it poses to fragile ecosystems.

In Brazil, photos from the Acampamento Terra Livre indigenous assembly highlight the gathering of indigenous peoples advocating for their rights and the demarcation of their territories.

The aftermath of Hurricane Barry brought devastating flooding to Louisiana, with images revealing the human and environmental toll of extreme weather events.

Fires raged in South Korea, captured in photographs that underscore the growing wildfire risks linked to climate change.

In the Philippines, a poignant shot depicts a crab entangled in plastic waste, illustrating the pervasive impact of ocean pollution on marine life.

The collapse of the Vale dam in Brazil is documented in harrowing images, exposing the dangers of mining operations and their effects on communities and landscapes.

In Indonesia, photographs of coal plant expansion reveal the ongoing push for fossil fuels and its consequences for local environments.

Across the world, the #FridaysForFuture student strikes are immortalized in images of young people marching and demanding urgent climate action from leaders.

Finally, Greenpeace's pole-to-pole ocean expedition is featured in photos that document research into threats like climate change and pollution, from the Arctic to the Antarctic.

These images from 2019 serve as a visual call to arms, showing the real-world impacts of environmental destruction and the determination of activists everywhere.
