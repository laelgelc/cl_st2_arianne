These fires are putting villages at risk and threatening precious wildlife, including sables, bears, deer, and elk that call this region home. Flying over the area in a helicopter, I saw the endless taiga forest stretching out below, now transformed into charred landscapes shrouded in thick smoke that hangs heavy in the air.

What's particularly alarming is that 90% of these fires are caused by human activity, often starting near logging sites and roads. They not only exacerbate climate change but are also intensified by it, creating a vicious cycle that allows the flames to spread further. Firefighters are working tirelessly, but the blazes have grown beyond human control, with smoke drifting to affect cities far from the fire zones.
