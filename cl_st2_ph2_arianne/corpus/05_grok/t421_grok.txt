Cabo Pulmo is a coral reef in Baja California Sur, Mexico, that hosts diverse marine life including fish, crustaceans, mollusks, birds, and mammals, many of which are at risk. Twenty-five years ago, the area was threatened by overfishing and irresponsible tourism. Local communities organized, which led to its declaration as a National Marine Park in 1995. As a result, fish populations rose over 400%, and migratory species such as whale sharks, mantas, humpback whales, turtles, and sharks returned. The communities shifted to ecotourism.
