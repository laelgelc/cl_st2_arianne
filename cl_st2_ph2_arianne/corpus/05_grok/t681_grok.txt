More than half of the world's population already lives in cities, and this figure is projected to reach 70% by 2050. As urban areas continue to grow, it's crucial that we prioritize people over cars to create healthier, more sustainable environments.

Cars bring a host of negative impacts to our cities: they generate noise and unpleasant smells, take up vast amounts of space, pose health risks through air pollution that kills 4.2 million people annually, and contribute significantly to climate harm. According to a Greenpeace report, we have only ten years left to phase out fossil fuel vehicles if we want to avert the worst effects of the climate crisis.

Imagine instead clean, connected, and community-focused cities that put people first.
