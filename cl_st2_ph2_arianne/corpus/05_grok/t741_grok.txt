I moved to Australia from a landlocked country at the age of 13, and ever since, I've been captivated by the ocean's mysteries. In the Great Australian Bight, I've witnessed incredible natural wonders that deserve our protection.

At Point Labatt Lookout, I watched endangered Australian sea lion pups as they played and learned in their coastal habitat. These young sea lions rely on fragile breeding sites that are highly sensitive to disturbances, making any intrusion a serious risk to their survival.

Among the underwater marvels, leafy sea dragons stand out, masterfully camouflaged in seagrass meadows. Yet, these unique creatures are endangered by pollution, which threatens their delicate existence.
