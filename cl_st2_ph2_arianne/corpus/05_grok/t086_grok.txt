In November, several positive environmental developments highlighted progress in the fight against climate change and pollution. Greece achieved a milestone by relying fully on renewable energy for five hours on October 5, demonstrating advancements in reducing dependence on fossil fuels. New York took a significant step forward with a ban on new gasoline-powered vehicles by 2035, which includes requirements for zero-emission vehicle sales to increase starting at 35% in 2026. Equinor announced a postponement of the Wisting oil field in the Arctic, a move that could prevent the project's development and avoid associated CO2 emissions. In Jakarta, residents secured a victory in a second appeal of an air pollution lawsuit, compelling the government to establish air quality standards and implement related strategies. Additionally, French President Macron voiced support for banning deep sea mining during COP27, bolstering global opposition to the activity.
