Amrekha Sharma has shared her reflections on the IPCC WGII report, which places a strong emphasis on justice in climate action. She highlights how the report validates the experiences of frontline communities and the value of Indigenous knowledge.

The report delves into vulnerabilities stemming from inequities, including those rooted in colonialism. It also examines intersectionality in the impacts on marginalized groups and stresses the importance of human rights-based approaches to adaptation.

Furthermore, the report supports climate litigation by centering diverse knowledge and historical contexts. It calls for accountability from governments and companies, while urging swift action to move beyond denial and embrace justice-focused responses.
