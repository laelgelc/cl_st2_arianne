In the world of environmental activism, it's always inspiring to spotlight the victories that show progress is possible. At Greenpeace, we're excited to share some recent positive developments that underscore the power of collective action and policy changes in protecting our planet.

One such win came in 2022 with the ban on bottom trawling in Swedish marine protected areas. This followed the strategic placement of boulders in 2009, which helped safeguard these vital ocean habitats from destructive fishing practices.

Meanwhile, the EU Parliament has taken a stand by passing a resolution opposing the East African Crude Oil Pipeline (EACOP), highlighting its serious risks to local communities and wildlife. This move aligns with the ongoing #StopEACOP campaign, amplifying voices calling for alternatives to such harmful projects.

In British Columbia, Indigenous groups are making strides in wildlife conservation by using maternity pens to help increase caribou populations, offering a hopeful model for species recovery.

On a global scale, the United Nations has declared a fundamental right to a healthy environment, setting an important precedent for environmental justice worldwide.

In Thailand, persistent campaigns and environmental assessments led to the cancellation of a planned coal plant in Krabi, preventing potential pollution and health impacts in the region.

Finally, courts in South Africa have revoked Shell's oil exploration rights off the coast, citing that they were granted illegally and would harm marine ecosystems. These decisions remind us that holding corporations accountable can yield real protections for our oceans.
