Deep beneath the ocean's surface, seamounts rise as underwater volcanic mountains, forming vibrant wildlife hotspots. These structures work by upwelling nutrients from the seafloor, drawing in diverse marine life. Many seamounts lie in the high seas, areas beyond national waters, where they encounter significant protection challenges. Regulatory gaps in these international zones are often exploited by destructive industries, leaving these ecosystems vulnerable.

At Greenpeace, we're actively campaigning for a global ocean treaty to safeguard precious ecosystems, including places like Mount Vema. As part of our pole-to-pole journey, we're conducting research into ocean biodiversity and the threats it faces.
