At Greenpeace, we're relentlessly campaigning for a toxic-free future, working to eliminate persistent, toxic, bioaccumulative, carcinogenic, and hormone-disrupting chemicals from our world. There are some hopeful signs on the horizon, such as increasing company transparency, the implementation of REACH regulations, and substitutions happening in various industriesâ€”though we know acceleration is urgently needed to make real progress.

To strengthen our efforts, we've just completed a two-year research project conducted by our Science Unit at Exeter University. This work reassessed our Detox strategy, with a sharp focus on key sectors including electronics, toys, cosmetics, household products, and textiles. The research zeroed in on six major chemical groups: PFCs, phthalates, heavy metals, BPA, BFRs, and APs/APEs.

Through this investigation, we compiled a comprehensive list of over 17,000 potentially hazardous chemicals. Our analysis of supply chains uncovered widespread toxic pollution, largely driven by a lack of transparency and information across these systems.

We're making this open research available to everyone, sharing it widely to support broader detoxification efforts and drive collective action toward a safer, cleaner planet.
