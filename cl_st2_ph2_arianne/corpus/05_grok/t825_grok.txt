Resolute Forest Products sued Greenpeace and Stand.Earth for advocating boreal forest protection, prompting global solidarity in over 25 countries. People gathered around iconic trees—like Indonesia's Kenari Babi, Lebanon's 3,000-year-old cedar, and Germany's beech forests—to oppose Resolute's efforts to silence critics and stifle free speech. Forests are vital for biodiversity and carbon storage to limit warming under 2°C. The post highlights nature's interconnected "web of life" and a united global movement defending forests and speech. It urges signing a petition for book publishers to support these causes.
