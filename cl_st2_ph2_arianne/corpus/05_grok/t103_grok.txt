Around the world, communities and activists are scoring vital wins for the planet. In a groundbreaking victory, Torres Strait Islanders secured a historic ruling from the United Nations against Australia's failure to act on climate change, marking a powerful step toward holding governments accountable.

In Europe, the EU has enacted a new law mandating that products such as cattle, cocoa, and palm oil must be free from links to deforestation, aiming to curb environmental destruction in supply chains.

Colombia has taken a crucial stand by ratifying the Escazú Agreement, which bolsters protections for environmental defenders facing threats in their work.

In Senegal, residents of Cayar have launched a lawsuit against a fishmeal factory accused of pollution, standing up for their local ecosystems and livelihoods.

Here at Greenpeace, our USA team successfully blocked a "dirty deal" that would have fast-tracked harmful fossil fuel projects, preventing further damage from dirty energy.

Positive changes are emerging in Russia too, where authorities have shrunk forest fire "zones of control" by 2 million square kilometers, potentially improving wildfire management and forest preservation.

And in New Zealand, a ban on single-use plastics has come into effect, with Greenpeace Aotearoa calling for even stronger measures to tackle plastic pollution. These developments show that momentum is building—let's keep pushing for a greener future.
