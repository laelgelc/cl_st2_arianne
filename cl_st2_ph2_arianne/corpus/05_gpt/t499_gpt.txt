The toll on wildlife is staggering. Over just a few months, about a billion animals are estimated to have been lost. This is not a distant statistic; it represents countless individual creatures that have perished in the flames or from hunger, thirst, and habitat loss in the aftermath. Species that were already vulnerable now face even greater pressure as their homes and food sources vanish.

These fires are not isolated disasters; they are a clear sign of a serious climate emergency. The scale and intensity of the destruction demand more than words or delayed promises. They demand immediate action that matches the urgency of what is unfolding: action to cut climate pollution, to protect and restore forests, and to support communities and wildlife on the frontlines of this crisis.
