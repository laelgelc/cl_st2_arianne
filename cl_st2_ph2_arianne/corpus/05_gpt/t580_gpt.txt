Greenpeace is mourning the recent passing of Dr. Roger Grace in New Zealand, a renowned underwater photographer whose work and spirit have left a lasting legacy within the organization and the wider ocean protection movement.

Dr. Grace first joined Greenpeace aboard the Rainbow Warrior II in 1990, taking part in its inaugural campaign against drift netters in the Tasman Sea. This early collaboration set the tone for many years of shared work at sea, where his images helped bring the hidden world beneath the waves to the surface of public consciousness.

Over the decades, he sailed on numerous Greenpeace expeditions, including voyages to Antarctica and the Pacific tuna campaign.
