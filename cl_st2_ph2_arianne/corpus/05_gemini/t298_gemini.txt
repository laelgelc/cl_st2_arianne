In 1995, community resistance began in Prachuab Khiri Khan (PKK), Thailand, against proposed coal-fired power plants. Locals were concerned about environmental impacts, a lack of transparency in Environmental Impact Assessments, and illegal land acquisition. They used non-violent tactics to oppose the projects, including protests, billboards, and signs.

Today, PKK remains coal-free. Its economy thrives on fishing, farming, and tourism. The community's resistance continues to inspire ongoing activism.
