Environmental justice requires social justice. Ocean advocates must address root causes such as racism, patriarchy, and inequalities to build a new normal where thriving is a right for all.

The stories of frontline communities have been erased. These include Senegalese women fish processors who are protesting industrial overfishing and transhipment, a practice linked to human rights abuses. They also include survivors of slavery in supply chains and marginalized groups affected by toxic facilities.

Greenpeace is committed to amplifying these voices and tackling systemic oppression. We are learning from current upheavals, including the pandemic and the demands for racial justice, to support those who are most impacted.
