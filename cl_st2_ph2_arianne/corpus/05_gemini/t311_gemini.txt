Indigenous Land Defenders in the Amazon face increasing threats to their lives and homes as they work to protect vital ecosystems. On May 26, illegal gold miners, who have been emboldened by Brazil's Bolsonaro administration, attacked a Munduruku village. The attack involved gunfire and arson, and resulted in the home of leader Maria Leusa being burned down.

This is not an isolated event but part of ongoing violence. In March, the headquarters of an Indigenous women's association was destroyed, and attacks on the Yanomami people have also occurred.
