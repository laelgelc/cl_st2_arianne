Under President Bolsonaro's three-year anti-environmental agenda, Brazil has faced devastating consequences. Deforestation in the Amazon has surged by 52.9%, while fires have ravaged the Cerrado and Pantanal biomes. In 2020 alone, the Pantanal lost a third of its area to these fires. This destruction has contributed to a 10% increase in Brazil's greenhouse gas emissions, positioning the country as the world's fifth-highest emitter.

This environmental crisis has been enabled by the deliberate weakening of key protective agencies. The budgets for IBAMA and ICMBio have been slashed by 30% and 32.7% respectively, and their staff has been reduced by 10%. This dismantling of environmental enforcement has fostered a climate of impunity, leading to increased violence against Indigenous peoples and environmental activists. The year 2021 saw 20 such killings, while 2020 was marked by 1,576 land conflicts.
