Across Asia, Greenpeace volunteers are united by a shared passion for climate justice and are sharing their inspiring stories of activism.

Ronan from the Philippines began his advocacy after Typhoon Haiyan, focusing on the power of storytelling and policy. In Malaysia, Hui Ling founded the local office and leads campaigns against pollution and deforestation. Tya from Indonesia serves on the front lines, fighting forest fires as a volunteer firefighter. From Thailand, Amika tackles the challenges of plastic waste and its climate impacts. After experiencing floods in South Korea, Minseop now designs visuals to push for climate policy change. In India, Neha promotes vital local solutions through composting and clean air initiatives.

Through their diverse work, these volunteers emphasize the power of resilience, the necessity of direct action, and the strength of community in protecting our environment.
