The urgent ecological crisis, highlighted by both activists and scientists, is undeniable. However, an effective response requires patience as a sign of respect for the sheer complexity of the situation, helping us to avoid simplistic solutions that may fail.

Drawing on the work of Donella Meadows and Russell Ackoff, we can see different approaches to this crisis. There are the inactivists, who do nothing; the reactivists, who attempt to return to a past state; and the preactivists, who try to predict and prepare for what is to come. A more effective path is that of the interactivists, who actively envision and work to create sustainable futures through deep commitment and a guiding vision.

This call for patience is aligned with Taoist principles of harmony, which can prevent the kind of backlash that hasty actions might provoke. Ultimately, urgency and patience are not opposites; they can and must coexist. This balance, much like the concept of yin and yang, enables thoughtful and effective action in the face of profound uncertainty. This perspective is supported by personal experience.
