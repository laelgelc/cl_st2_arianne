During the COVID-19 pandemic, Jen Maman reflects on feeling safe at home with her daughter, while also sharing the experiences of her colleagues around the world. For these colleagues, safety is defined as having access to clean water, air, and food. It also means fair healthcare, a government that addresses inequalities, and meaningful climate action.

This perspective critiques excessive military spending. For instance, a single $3.4 billion submarine could fund 30 hospitals. The US spends six times more on defense than it does on education, even as global peace is declining.

The post advocates for redirecting funds from the military to things that create true safety. In the face of crises like pandemics, police violence, and environmental disasters, this means investing in healthcare, clean energy, and community support.
