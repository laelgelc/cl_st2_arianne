After running into overtime negotiations, the 27th UN Climate Change Conference has concluded. A key victory was the establishment of a Loss and Damage fund, which will aid communities impacted by the climate crisis. This achievement, however, came amid significant disappointments.

Despite calls from many countries, no agreement was reached on a fossil fuel phase-out. This failure now increases the pressure for action at COP28.
