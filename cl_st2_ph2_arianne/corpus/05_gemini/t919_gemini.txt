In 2015, a movement of seven million people helped halt Shell's plans for Arctic expansion. This was followed by the Paris climate agreement, which signaled a global shift away from fossil fuels and towards a future of sustainability and environmental protection.

The Arctic, however, is warming at twice the rate of the rest of the planet. This January saw a record low for sea ice. As the ice melts, it is enabling destructive industrial activity. In the Barents Sea, trawlers are moving into previously inaccessible areas, risking habitats, causing bycatch, and damaging fragile ecosystems.
