A photo editor has described the significant mental health toll that comes from viewing a constant stream of images depicting global climate disasters. These events, exacerbated by climate change, are unfolding worldwide.

Recent examples include the devastating floods in Pakistan, which have submerged a third of the country and displaced millions of people, despite the nation's low emissions. In Bangalore, India, unprecedented rains caused widespread damage, a situation made worse by poor planning. Across West and Central Africa, flooding has displaced more than 125,000 people and led to 300 deaths in Nigeria.

Meanwhile, Europe has been experiencing its worst drought, causing rivers to dry up. In Chile, an ongoing drought, worsened by the privatization of water, now affects half of the country's population. This is all happening as typhoons and hurricanes have also recently hit Asia, Puerto Rico, and Canada.

Amid the frustration over profits being made from fossil fuels, there is a source of hope. Youth marches are taking place, signaling a growing desire to end the dependence on them.
