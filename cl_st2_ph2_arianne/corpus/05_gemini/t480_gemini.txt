Greenpeace celebrates International Women’s Day by highlighting female visual storytellers who are passionate about environmental issues. The profiles include Arati Kumar-Rao, who documents South Asia’s rivers and biodiversity, and Khadija Farah, who photographs the impacts of climate change on Zanzibar’s seaweed farmers.

Maki Hayashida explores sustainable Japanese landscapes, while Ore Huiying focuses on communities along the Mekong River. Eva Verbeeck captures Africa’s climate crisis as well as water scarcity in the U.S. Sarah Grohnert’s work involves filming the bonds between Maori people and their land. The features also include Priya Thuvassery, who directs films about Indian coral reefs, and Teresa Camou Guerrero, who documents Mexican corn farmers. Also highlighted are Tali Kimelman, who focuses on forest bathing, and Morgan Trimble, who covers African wildlife conservation.
