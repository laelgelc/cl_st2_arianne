A biography, *Mr. Mindbomb*, details the life of Greenpeace co-founder Robert Lorne Hunter, who lived from 1941 to 2005. The book is edited by his wife, Bobbi Hunter, and is composed of accounts from friends, family, and colleagues. It covers his early life, his career in journalism, and his pacifist influences.

The biography outlines his crucial role in the formation of Greenpeace. This includes his involvement in campaigns against nuclear testing and whaling, which utilized "mindbomb" tactics. Contributors to the book remember him for his humility, humor, and his role as an inspiration in global ecology actions.

After his time at Greenpeace, Hunter worked as an ecology reporter. His enduring legacy is also present in his books, such as *2030: Thermageddon*. The biography concludes with reflections from his family, which touch upon his passing and his spirit of positive change.
