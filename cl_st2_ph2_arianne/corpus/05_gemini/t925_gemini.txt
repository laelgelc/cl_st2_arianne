As part of the Detox Outdoor campaign, Greenpeace surveyed major outdoor brands about their use of PFCs, revealing widespread chemical contamination in their products.

Over 30,000 people voted to help select 40 items for testing. Of those tested, PFCs were detected in all but four products. Notably, high levels of hazardous long-chain PFCs and PFOA were found in gear from The North Face and Mammut.

These persistent chemicals are known to pollute remote natural areas. They can enter food chains, affecting both wildlife and humans.
