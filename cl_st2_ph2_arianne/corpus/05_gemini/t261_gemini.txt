For 50 years, creative activism and art have been integrated into Greenpeace's mission. This began with the very first voyage, when peace and ecology symbols were painted on the sails of the Phyllis Cormack. The ship sailed to protest U.S. nuclear testing in Amchitka, an action which led to the cancellation of future tests.

After returning in 2020, Naegeli donated some of his drawings for an auction celebrating Greenpeace's 50th anniversary. His contribution inspired the global Hope through Action mural project. Taking place in 12 cities, the project aims to encourage public involvement in activism, whether through signing petitions, writing letters, or joining protests.
