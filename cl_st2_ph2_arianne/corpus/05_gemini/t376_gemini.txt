The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the fragility of life. It is a moment that calls for immediate care for Mother Earth to prevent new pandemics, which can arise from the destruction of biodiversity.

Indigenous Peoples have long warned that human life is interconnected with nature. They caution that predatory economic models put everyone's future at risk. Since the time of colonial invasions, Indigenous groups in Brazil have adapted to fight for their rights, using the Constitution as their tool.

The Articulation of Indigenous Peoples of Brazil (APIB), together with allies that include Greenpeace Brazil and political parties, has filed a case with the Supreme Court. The action seeks to compel the government to protect the Amazon, its forests, its waters, and all forms of life.
