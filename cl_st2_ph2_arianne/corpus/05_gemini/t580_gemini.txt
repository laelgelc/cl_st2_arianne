Greenpeace announced the recent passing of Dr. Roger Grace, a renowned underwater photographer who collaborated extensively with the organization in New Zealand.

His work with Greenpeace began in 1990, when he joined the Rainbow Warrior II's inaugural campaign against drift netters in the Tasman Sea. Over the years, he participated in numerous other expeditions, including to Antarctica and the Pacific tuna campaign.

As a classical photographer, he used minimal equipment to create iconic underwater images. His work reflected a deep love for marine life and a dedication to its protection.
