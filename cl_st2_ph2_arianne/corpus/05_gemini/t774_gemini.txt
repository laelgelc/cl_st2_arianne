Climate change disproportionately impacts women, empowering them to lead the climate justice movement through storytelling and activism.

Joanna Sustento, a survivor of Typhoon Haiyan who lost family members, advocates for holding carbon companies accountable. She emphasizes that having a safe environment is a human right. Desiree Llanos Dee uses her personal stories to humanize the effects of climate change, connect global communities, and build collective action.

Greenpeace sailor Hettie amplifies the voices of the vulnerable through ship tours, such as a visit to Tacloban. The resilience of women is celebrated, and there is a call to support their fight against climate injustice.
