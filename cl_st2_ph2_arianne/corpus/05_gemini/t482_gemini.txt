Governments are failing to meet their Paris Agreement climate promises, a reality that is prompting thousands of people to seek justice through the courts. In response, the legal profession is mobilizing. The International Bar Association has created a Model Statute to aid judges and lawyers in assessing evidence and enforcing the rights of people to challenge governments and corporations. Greenpeace Southeast Asia is also contributing by offering a guide for public accountability.

Globally, over 600 climate-related cases have been filed. Notable examples include Urgenda's victory in the Dutch Supreme Court and the Klimaseniorinnen appeal in Switzerland. The fight extends to the corporate world, as seen in the Philippines, where companies like Exxon are being held liable for climate impacts.
