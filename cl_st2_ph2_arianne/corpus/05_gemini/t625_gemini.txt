A group of women photographers are dedicated to documenting environmental and humanitarian issues worldwide. Their work brings attention to critical situations across the globe, from human rights to ecological crises.

Suzanne Lee exposes the conditions of child labor in Indian coal mines, while Hilina Abebe captures the stories of climate change in Ethiopia. In the planet’s most extreme environments, Acacia Johnson focuses on the polar regions. Tamara Merino highlights the desert blooms and human isolation in the Atacama, and Gayatri Ganju weaves together narratives of humanity and nature.

In the Peruvian Amazon, Ann Wang documents the impact of oil spills. Diana Zeyneb Alhindawi addresses the intersection of conservation and poverty. Ellen Cuylaerts photographs marine life, and Jessica Suarez advocates for the preservation of the Amazon. In Ecuador’s Yasuni National Park, Karla Gachet portrays the lives of its Indigenous peoples.

Featured in major outlets, their collective work uses the power of imagery to inspire action and challenge inaction.
