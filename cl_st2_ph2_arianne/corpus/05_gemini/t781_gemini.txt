The 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics has made a commitment to renewables, with six of its venues powered by clean energy and a target for zero emissions. This action is set against the backdrop of climate change's impacts on winter sports, where melting snow is forcing athletes to find new training sites. A study has found that nine past and future Olympic cities may soon be too warm to host the games.

Despite the environmental focus of the games, sponsor Samsung has seen its emissions rise by 55% since 2010 and uses only 1% renewables. This record contrasts with their taglines. We must urge Samsung's CEOs to shift to clean energy in order to combat the climate crisis.
