Women and gender non-conforming individuals are vital agents in climate action, yet they are frequently excluded from decision-making. A need for inclusive shifts is also highlighted by the issue of eco-ableism, which creates distrust between disabled communities and environmental groups.

Profiles of activists demonstrate this essential leadership. Archana Soreng focuses on indigenous knowledge and the protection of forests. Eunbin Kang is involved in youth resistance to coal, and Hasminah D. Paudac-Tawano engages in legal fights against polluters. Liu Junyan works on climate risks and inequality, while Pua Lay Peng is dedicated to combating plastic waste. Sylvia Wu is active in climate litigation and fishery rights. Veronica Cabe's work centers on community organizing. Yaewon Hwang addresses trans justice, and Ai Ji focuses on threats to biodiversity.
