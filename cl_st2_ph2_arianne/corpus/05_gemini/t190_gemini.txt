Record-breaking temperatures have been recorded in Antarctica, reaching 40°C above normal, while areas near the North Pole have seen temperatures 30°C above normal. These are signals of rapid climate change, with impacts like heatwaves, wildfires, and floods affecting communities across the world.

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) is scheduled to release its third working group report on mitigation on April 4. This report will cover progress on emission reductions and detail pathways that are compatible with the goals of the Paris Agreement. It will also address social factors related to consumption and examine options in sectors such as energy, agriculture, transport, and industry.

There is a need for a rapid transition from fossil fuels. Looking ahead to COP27 and the upcoming October 2022 Synthesis Report, action must be based on science.
