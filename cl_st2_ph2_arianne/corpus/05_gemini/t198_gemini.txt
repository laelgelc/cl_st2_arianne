The climate crisis disproportionately impacts women in the Global South, with those in Africa facing significant challenges. These women are often excluded from decision-making while their livelihoods in fishing and farming are under threat.

In Senegal, women fish processors are actively protesting against industrial trawlers that are stealing fish from their communities. Their fight is part of a larger movement where advocates are emphasizing the importance of feminism, decoloniality, and the intersections of climate, gender, racial, and economic justice.

There is a call for paradigm shifts and for the inclusion of LGBTIQ people in climate relief efforts. Highlighting the leadership from the continent, six African women who are leading climate conversations are being profiled. These women are recognized for their crucial roles in building resilience, sharing traditional knowledge, and fighting inequality.
