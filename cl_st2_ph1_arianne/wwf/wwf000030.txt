We’re surrounded by nature and not just when we’re in the park or swimming in our favorite lake. It’s the oxygen we breathe and water we drink, but it’s also providing for us in ways we often take for granted or may not even know.
Every car tire comes from natural rubber, which, of course, comes from trees. In fact, 75% of the world’s rubber is used to make tires alone . But rubber is at risk if we can’t stop rampant deforestation and forest degradation.
Whenever you turn on a light, adjust your thermostat, or plug in a device, you’re getting a charge from nature. A great deal of our electricity comes from power generated by freshwater sources.
Every day, no matter what we are doing, we are surrounded by the things that nature provides for us. For example, key ingredients in most haircare products come to us thanks to our freshwater rivers and streams.
Tattered and torn or pressed to perfection, your favorite cotton t-shirt took 713 gallons of water to manufacture. That’s enough H2O to sustain one person for 900 days. And that’s before you wash it: A load of laundry uses another 40 gallons of water.
The demand for nature and its resources continues to climb and we’ve seen its impact: Drastic declines in biodiversity and loss of critical habitats and resources. For nature to continue to thrive and provide, we must do our part. Because nature needs us now .
